Limited Range for Passive Tags
Passive RFID tags need to be very close (a few meters) to the reader to work, which makes it unreliable for moving buses unless they slow down or stop near the reader.

High Cost for Large Deployment
Every bus stop would require a separate RFID reader setup, power source, and network link â€” making it expensive and difficult to maintain city-wide.

Complex Installation & Maintenance
Readers need precise alignment, proper antenna placement, and weatherproof casing. This setup becomes complicated and less practical for outdoor public stops.

Poor Performance at Vehicle Speed
Passive RFID signals may not activate long enough when the bus passes at normal traffic speed, leading to missed reads and inaccurate tracking.

Cheaper, More Reliable Alternatives Exist
Bluetooth beacons, GPS-assisted systems, or driver app triggers can provide the same tracking accuracy with lower cost, easier setup, and better scalability.
